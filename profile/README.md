## How to Claim the $300 Credit

The sequence here matters more than most pages acknowledge.

**Step 1: Enter through the promotional link, not Vultr.com directly.**
Credits often apply automatically at the entry point. Going to Vultr.com first and then looking for a promo code later can mean the credit does not apply. Use the right link from the start.

**Step 2: Create a new account.**
This offer is for first-time Vultr accounts only. It must be a fresh email address with no previous Vultr account history.

**Step 3: Complete billing setup.**
Vultr requires a valid credit or debit card to activate the account. You are not charged during the credit period, but the payment method is mandatory. That is standard for every serious cloud provider. Anyone claiming otherwise is not being straight with you.

**Step 4: Confirm the credit appears.**
On the billing summary page, the $300 should be listed automatically. If it is missing, enter the promotional code manually before completing setup.

**Step 5: Deploy with intention.**
The 30-day clock starts at account creation, not at first deployment. Every day you wait is a day of credit window gone. Have a clear test plan before you sign up.

## What to Test During the 30 Days

Go in with a specific checklist. Vague exploration wastes the credit window.

**Deployment speed.** How long does it take to spin up a fresh instance in your target region? Vultr typically provisions cloud compute instances in under 60 seconds. Verify this yourself.

**Dashboard usability.** Can you navigate billing, firewall rules, snapshots, and DNS management without reaching for the documentation? The answer should be yes within the first hour.

**API reliability.** If you plan to automate deployments, test the Vultr API during the trial. The documentation is thorough, but reliability under load is something you verify, not assume.

**Regional performance.** Deploy in two or three data center locations and run latency tests against your actual audience. Pick the region that wins.

**Support response time.** Submit a non-urgent support ticket during the trial. The speed and quality of the response tells you more than any review site.

If the platform passes these tests, you have your answer. If it fails one of them in a way that matters to your use case, you have learned something valuable without paying anything to find out.

## Is Vultr Worth Paying For After the Trial?

For developers, site owners, and technical teams who need reliable cloud infrastructure with global reach, yes. Vultr competes well at its price point.

If you want a provider to handle your database, email, and application layer with minimal technical involvement, Vultr requires more self-management than platforms like Cloudways or managed WordPress hosts. That is a real tradeoff, and the trial is the right place to figure out whether it is one you can live with.
